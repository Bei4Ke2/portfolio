I am a designer who aspires to bring change through intuitive designs and delightful experiences. The process of problem-solving has always been intuitive to me, and I am energized by the difficulties problems present. Over the years, I have had the opportunity to work in different industries and partake in unique roles, and I believe my diverse experience has made me a humble person who is equipped with skills that are crucial for a designer. As an effective communicator and an empathetic problem solver, I’m passionate about speaking the user’s language and creating solutions that address the user's pain points. My background in economics and management consulting has taught me the importance of business goals and key drivers of success, allowing me to achieve harmony by building products with both user and company goals in mind.

How did I get here?

Starting at the age of 12, I became aware of my interests and appreciation for well-designed computer games. I have always loved learning about various game models and experiencing a variety of game journeys and interface/dashboard designs. I did not think much of it at the time while my parents were not exactly the encouraging type when it came to gaming. Time flew, and life went on. It wasn't until much later when I thought about how a game is a well-thought-out product that heavily employs all aspects of experience design and how incredible it would be to be part of designing an enjoyable experience for people.

During my free time, I like to dance, snowboard, work on jigsaw puzzles, play computer games, and volunteer at CoachArt where I teach children how to hip-hop dance.

I look forward to joining a team that will lead and shape the future of experience design.
